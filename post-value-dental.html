Value Dental of Reno: The Clinic That Ghosted Me

Yes, my teeth needing to be removed was partly on me. I can own that. What I won’t own is the absolute bullshit of being abandoned mid-treatment by a dental office that was supposed to help me.

This is my honest experience with Value Dental of Reno in Reno, Nevada — from a good first impression to getting my teeth pulled and then being completely ignored when I needed dentures and follow-up care.

Clinic Info (So You Know Exactly Who I’m Talking About)

Office Name: Value Dental of Reno

Address: 175 Brinkby Avenue, Reno, NV 89509

Phone: (775) 446-7623

Website: valuedentalreno.com

Posted Hours (at time of writing):
Monday–Thursday: 8:00 AM – 3:00 PM · Friday–Sunday: Closed

I am sharing my personal experience so others can make informed choices. What follows is exactly what happened to me, in my own words.

Owning My Part (Because I’m Not Here to Pretend)

Let me start with this: I know my teeth didn’t end up in bad shape overnight. I’m not pretending I did everything right. Years of stress, kids, mental health, survival mode, and putting myself last all caught up to me. By the time I went in, it was past the point of simple fixes.

So yes — needing my top teeth removed was partly my fault. I take responsibility for that. What I don’t take responsibility for is what this office did next.

The Consultation That Actually Felt Hopeful

In September, I went to Value Dental of Reno to see what my options were. The dentist was actually super nice. He was kind, explained things clearly, and I genuinely felt like I was being listened to instead of judged.

We went over everything and landed on a plan:

  • All of my top teeth needed to be removed.
  • I would eventually have a partial on the bottom.
  • Because I had an implant tooth, I needed to see an oral surgeon.

The office sent the referral, and the plan was simple: I’d go to the oral surgeon, see what they could do, then call Value Dental back and we’d move forward with impressions for dentures.

At this point, everything seemed fine. I was nervous, but hopeful. It felt like, “Okay, this sucks, but at least we have a plan.”

The Surgery & the Delayed Denture Plan

I went to the oral surgeon, talked through options, and decided to go with delayed dentures.

Why delayed? Because:

  • I have three kids.
  • Finding childcare for multiple appointments is a whole Olympic event.
  • I needed something that made sense for my actual life, not some ideal schedule.

On September 30th, I had all of my top teeth removed. It was painful. It was emotional. It was a big deal. But I did it, because I thought we had a full plan in place and that I wouldn’t be left hanging.

And Then… They Just Stopped Answering

As soon as I could talk like a semi-normal human again, I did what I was supposed to do: I called Value Dental to schedule my impressions for dentures.

They didn’t answer.

Okay, people get busy. Offices have off days. I left space. I called again.

Still no answer. No call back. No “Hey, we’re swamped, we’ll get you in soon.” Nothing.

I kept trying. I left time between calls. I wasn’t calling every five minutes — I was giving them the benefit of the doubt. Meanwhile, I’m healing from major oral surgery, missing all my top teeth, waiting for the next step of a process they helped set up.

And they were just… gone.

Ghosted by a Dental Office

Weeks went by. No response. No callback. No “We’re behind.” No “We need to reschedule.” Just silence.

Then, about a month later, I call again and hear a new voicemail saying they’re now only open two days a week.

Cool. Great. But here’s the thing: even if you change your hours, you don’t get to ignore patients who are literally mid-treatment and depending on you to function like an actual healthcare office.

As of writing this, it’s been close to two months. I have not heard back from them. Not a single returned call. Not a follow-up. Nothing.

Why This Isn’t “Just Inconvenient” — It’s Harmful

This isn’t like a nail salon never calling you back about an appointment. This is dental care. This is someone’s mouth, health, confidence, ability to eat, and ability to exist in public without feeling like a walking before-and-after photo.

I followed the plan. I went to the surgeon. I got the extractions. I did the hard part. And then the office that I was supposed to go back to completely disappeared on me.

That’s more than just unprofessional. It’s cruel. It left me in limbo, trying to figure out next steps with no guidance from the people who were supposed to be part of my care team.

Would I Recommend Value Dental of Reno?

Absolutely not.

I don’t care how nice the website looks or what their hours say or how friendly someone might seem the first time. My experience was this:

  • Good first visit.
  • Clear plan made.
  • Major dental work done.
  • Then completely ghosted when I tried to move forward with dentures.

I am sharing my story because I don’t want anyone else to be blindsided the way I was. You deserve a dental office that answers the phone, calls you back, and doesn’t vanish once things get complicated.

If you’re in Reno and looking for dental care, my honest opinion is this: for your own sanity, your own healing, and your own safety — go literally anywhere else.
